SHOULD I
CLOSE MY APPS?

Short answer:

NO

Except GPS Apps

Wish your battery would last longer? Lots of people do! And some people go as far as to say that you have to keep closing all the apps on your iPhone or iPad. Truth is: you don't need to.

In iOS, though, most Apps aren't even running in the background most of the time! When they're not running, they're simply frozen. They're loaded, just never running or eating battery.

Here's how Apps can fake being running in the background:

Local Notifications

Apps can ask the OS to pop a notification at X time in the future even if they're frozen by then. They appear with the App's icon and everything, but come from the OS. If you slide or tap the notification, the App gets brought back from the fridge.

Don't close!

App won't even be running.

Push Notifications

Apps can arrange with their own services to offload the work they'd be doing in the background to their servers — like always being logged into IM services for you. This way the Apps can go to the fridge and stop eating your battery. Push notifications are how their services can notify you that there's new content for you.

Don't close!

App won't even be running.

But sometimes Apps really need some background time to do work for you.
iOS allows them to do so following some pretty strict rules:

10-second rule

By default all Apps in iOS, when "homed away" from, get 10 seconds and then they're frozen. Most Apps are like this — games in particular. This happens when they don't explicitly request any extra background time from the OS.

Don't close!

Very short background life.

10-minute rule

An App can request additional background time the moment you "home away" from them. These Apps get 10 minutes and then they're frozen. Any App can do this, but most don't. Apps usually do this when they're finishing an upload or a download and if, by the time it reaches 10 minutes of background time, the App isn't done, it gets punched in the face and thrown in the fridge.

Apps that request extra background time usually do so to process or upload/download stuff. Fortunately they are usually short-lived and finish way before the 10-minute mark.

Don't close!

Short background life.

Audio rule

Audio Apps are allowed to remain running in the background as long as they're playing audio. Once paused, "homing away" from them freezes them.

These Apps usually only do audio when in the background. Sure, they can stay on for a long time, but your iPhone nowadays is rated at 40 hours of audio playback. I wouldn't worry about these.

One exception to this rule is if the App streams audio from the internet like Pandora. If you're streaming audio, then you're using either cellular data or WiFi. Streaming + audio simultaneously should bring you down to somewhere between 7 and 10 hours on a battery charge depending on the quality of the stream and signal.

Don't close!

Pause the music.

Geofence rule

Geofence Apps stay on-and-off in the background. Whenever your device finds its location has changed significantly, it calls up the App for it to do it's background magic. The App gets a little time and then it's back in the fridge.

Using a geofence doesn't necessarily use the GPS on your device, but it might have to, if you have little to no cell signal where you are. These Apps will incur some battery hit while using a geofence, but it's low enough that might be very worthwhile to some people.

If you don't want these Apps eating away at your battery, you should disable their geofence features in the App, otherwise everytime the App is run, its geofence will get set up and start eating away at your battery again. Closing it won't help.

Don't close!

Disable geofenced actions.

VoIP rule

VoIP Apps are allowed to remain running in the background as long as they're connected to their services. Once disconnected, "homing away" from them freezes them.

This will hurt your battery because these apps will keep extra data connections open in order to keep you signed on.

Don't close!

Sign-off instead.

GPS rule

GPS Apps get to be in the background all the time in order to provide proper directions to the user. These usually need to be closed (either from the App itself or from the Multitasking Tray) in order to stop running in the background.

Using the GPS radio is one of the most power-hungry things you can do on your device. Think of it this way: it's talking to a freaking satellite in space!

CLOSE!

That's basically it! Apps do get some background time, but they're fiercely controlled.

More important than worrying about what is doing work in the background, is to worry about what you do that consumes more or less battery.
In a rough order from most to less power-hungry:

  • GPS localization
  • Very bright screen on for long periods
  • Cellular data transfers
  • WiFi data transfers
  • Being in bad cellular coverage
  • Video playback
  • Audio playback
